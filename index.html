OpenCivix is a modular election intelligence platform that integrates social media monitoring, field reporting, verification systems, and analytical tools to support election-related situational awareness, transparency, and structured review.

Initial Capability: Social Media Monitoring Module

The first operational component of OpenCivix focuses on social media monitoring as a structured intake and analysis layer. This module is designed to capture, classify, and organize public election-related signals for downstream verification and review.

Signal Capture

Tracks public election-related content across digital platforms using configurable keyword logic, topic groupings, and monitoring rules.

Classification

Applies initial categorization to content and actors, including misinformation patterns, narrative themes, and behavioral signals.

Structured Intake

Converts captured signals into standardized event records that can be reviewed, validated, and integrated into broader platform workflows.

What Distinguishes OpenCivix

OpenCivix is designed to move beyond basic monitoring by combining signal detection, verification, election-specific categorization, and structured intelligence production within a single platform.

Hybrid Intelligence Model

Combines social media monitoring, observer reporting, and public submissions to create a broader and more corroborated view of election-related activity.

Verification as a Core Function

Treats credibility assessment, cross-validation, and review workflows as central design features rather than secondary processes applied after collection.

Modular architecture for election intelligence.

The platform is structured as interoperable layers that convert distributed inputs into organized analytical outputs suitable for monitoring, documentation, and structured review.

Multi-Source Data Ingestion

Aggregates inputs from social platforms, messaging applications, observer forms, and citizen-submitted reports. Inputs are normalized into a common event structure, including timestamp, location, actor type, and event category.

AI Classification Layer

Applies multi-signal analysis to classify content and actors, including electoral misinformation, voter suppression narratives, automated behavior, coordinated activity, and other disruptive patterns.

Network and Coordination Detection

Identifies amplification networks, narrative clusters, and coordinated campaigns, supporting alert generation and influence mapping across monitored environments.

Verification Engine

Combines automated scoring, duplication checks, bot-likelihood indicators, human review, and cross-source validation to support credibility production and traceability.

Election-Specific Data Model

Organizes events according to election-relevant categories such as voter registration, campaign activity, media environment, polling operations, counting, tabulation, results, and disputes.

Transparency and Access Layers

Supports both public-facing transparency views and restricted analytical environments, including dashboards, incident maps, verification status, and investigative tools.
